# 05 – Planning fallacy

**Category:** A – Estimation and forecasting

## Definition

Systematically underestimating the time, cost, and risk of your own projects while knowing that similar projects routinely overrun.

## Impact on decisions

Launch dates get committed on best-case task lists. Dependencies, rework, approvals, and vacations vanish from the plan. Downstream teams build commitments on dates that were never realistic.

## Detection signals

- Timeline built bottom-up from tasks with no buffer
- Past overruns of similar projects not referenced in the plan
- The best-case estimate becomes the committed date

## Countermeasures

- Use reference class forecasting: how long did the last three similar projects take?
- Add explicit buffers and state them; do not hide them inside tasks
- Run a premortem: "It is six months later and we shipped late – why?"
- Commit ranges externally, not point dates, until risk burns down

## Marketing example

A website relaunch is planned for 8 weeks based on the task list. The previous three relaunches each took 5-6 months. The 8-week date gets announced at sales kickoff. The site launches half-finished, with broken tracking, in month four.
