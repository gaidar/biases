# 42 – Self-serving bias

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

Attributing wins to your own skill and losses to bad luck or external factors.

## Impact on decisions

Organizational learning stops. Postmortems on losses blame the market; retrospectives on wins credit the team. Since causes are never honestly identified, the same mistakes repeat while the same false lessons compound.

## Detection signals

- Loss postmortems dominated by external factors, win reviews by internal ones
- No pre-registered predictions to check attributions against
- Identical mistakes recurring across quarters

## Countermeasures

- Apply symmetric analysis: list internal and external factors for every outcome, win or loss
- Record predictions and reasoning before outcomes arrive; review against them
- Have someone uninvolved lead the postmortem

## Marketing example

When campaigns beat target, readouts credit creative excellence and sharp targeting. When they miss, readouts cite "market conditions" and "seasonality." Over a year, the same channel-selection mistake appears in four missed campaigns – never identified, because the misses were always the market's fault.
