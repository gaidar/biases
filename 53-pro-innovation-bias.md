# 53 – Pro-innovation bias

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

Assuming new technology should be adopted everywhere, with novelty itself treated as justification. The costs, fit, and failure modes of the new thing get underexamined.

## Impact on decisions

Tools get bought for what they represent rather than what they solve. Adoption precedes problem definition. Budgets fund being modern instead of being effective.

## Detection signals

- Adoption justified by "this is the future" rather than a named problem
- No total cost of ownership or fit analysis before purchase
- Prior-generation tools abandoned before the new one is proven

## Countermeasures

- Start from the problem: what specifically is broken, and what would fixing it be worth?
- Pilot with kill criteria and a defined success metric before rollout
- Include integration, training, and maintenance in the cost, not just the license

## Marketing example

The team buys an AI personalization engine because "AI is the future of web experience." The site gets 8,000 monthly visitors – far too little traffic for the models to learn segment behavior. $75K per year produces no measurable lift, and the vendor's case studies all came from sites with fifty times the traffic.
