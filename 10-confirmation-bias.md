# 10 – Confirmation bias

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Seeking, noticing, and weighting evidence that supports what you already believe, while ignoring or discounting evidence against it.

## Impact on decisions

Research gets designed to confirm decisions already made. Dashboards get filtered to the metrics that look good. Contrary data arrives late, diluted, or not at all, so bad strategies survive far longer than they should.

## Detection signals

- Readouts contain only supportive data points
- Survey questions written to produce the desired answer
- Contrary evidence explained away faster than supportive evidence is scrutinized

## Countermeasures

- Define what evidence would change your mind before collecting data
- Assign someone to argue the opposite case with equal preparation
- Pre-register success criteria for campaigns and tests
- Actively search for the strongest disconfirming data, not the weakest

## Marketing example

The team believes the rebrand drove pipeline growth. The readout includes only post-rebrand wins and a brand-lift survey with leading questions. It omits that sales headcount doubled in the same period. The next budget cycle funds a brand refresh instead of the sales enablement that actually moved the number.
