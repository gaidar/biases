# 47 – Bias blind spot

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

Recognizing biases in others while believing yourself to be objective. Knowing about biases does not exempt you from them – it often just improves your ability to diagnose them in colleagues.

## Impact on decisions

Debiasing processes get applied to other people's decisions and waived for one's own. The largest, most senior-owned decisions – usually the highest-stakes ones – receive the least scrutiny.

## Detection signals

- "I'm data-driven; they're biased" in any form
- Checklists and reviews mandated for the team, skipped by the mandator
- The biggest budget items facing the least structured challenge

## Countermeasures

- Apply decision processes universally, with seniority increasing scrutiny rather than reducing it
- Invite external critique of your own decisions specifically
- Assume you are as biased as everyone else and rely on process, not self-awareness

## Marketing example

The CMO mandates debiasing checklists for the team's media plans, then exempts his own creative judgments as "experience." The largest single budget item – his long-standing sponsorship – is the least scrutinized line in the plan, and has never faced the ROI review required of every $20K campaign.
