# 34 – Halo effect

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

One positive trait – fame, polish, a big-name client – colors judgment of everything else about a person, vendor, or brand. The mirror image (horn effect) works the same way with one negative trait.

## Impact on decisions

Vendors get hired on reputation rather than fit. Impressive presentation skills get mistaken for strategic competence. One strong dimension suspends scrutiny of all others.

## Detection signals

- "They did great work for [famous brand]" as the deciding argument
- Evaluation criteria collapse into an overall impression
- Hard questions postponed because the counterparty is impressive

## Countermeasures

- Score each evaluation criterion separately before forming an overall view
- Check references specifically relevant to your use case, not the famous ones
- Assign someone to assess the weakest dimension, not the strongest

## Marketing example

An agency with a famous consumer campaign wins the B2B account despite having no B2B references. Deliverables are beautiful and generate no pipeline. The halo delayed hard questions about demand-gen capability by two quarters and roughly $400K in fees.
