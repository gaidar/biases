# 02 – Availability heuristic

**Category:** A – Estimation and forecasting

## Definition

Judging how likely or common something is by how easily examples come to mind, rather than by actual frequency.

## Impact on decisions

Recent, dramatic, or personally experienced events dominate planning. Risks that made headlines get overweighted; quiet, frequent problems get ignored. Channel and tactic choices follow whatever was memorable, not what the data shows.

## Detection signals

- "Everyone is talking about X" used as evidence of importance
- Strategy shifts right after a conference or a viral competitor moment
- No frequency data behind claims about what customers do or want

## Countermeasures

- Pull actual frequency data before deciding; treat anecdotes as hypotheses
- Keep a decision journal so vivid recent events do not overwrite the record
- Ask: "Is this common, or just easy for me to remember?"

## Marketing example

A competitor's TikTok video goes viral. Within two weeks the team shifts 30% of paid budget to TikTok. Their buyers are IT directors at mid-market firms who are not on the platform in a buying context. Pipeline contribution stays near zero for two quarters while search budget, which was converting, got cut.
