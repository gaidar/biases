# 51 – Parkinson's law of triviality

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

Groups spend disproportionate time on trivial, easily understood topics and rush the complex, high-stakes ones – because everyone can have an opinion about the bike shed and few can about the reactor.

## Impact on decisions

Big decisions get made by default, offline, or by whoever moves first, while meetings consume hours on details that do not matter. Time allocation inverts against impact.

## Detection signals

- Forty minutes on button color, five minutes on channel strategy
- High-stakes items "taken offline" and never properly decided
- Everyone participating vigorously in low-stakes debates, silent on high-stakes ones

## Countermeasures

- Time-box agenda items in proportion to their financial impact
- Put the biggest decision first, when energy and time exist
- Delegate genuinely trivial choices to one owner; do not bring them to groups

## Marketing example

The launch review spends most of its hour debating booth carpet color and the event hashtag. The unresolved $500K media-mix question gets "taken offline" in the last three minutes and is eventually decided by default – last year's mix, again – because time ran out and no one reconvened.
