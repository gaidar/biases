# 52 – Surrogation

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

The metric replaces the goal it was meant to represent. People optimize the number instead of the outcome, and eventually forget the two were ever different.

## Impact on decisions

Metrics improve while the business does not. Incentives tied to proxies produce gaming: the measured thing gets manufactured, the valuable thing gets neglected. Goodhart's law in daily operation.

## Detection signals

- The tracked metric rising while revenue or pipeline stays flat
- Tactics that obviously inflate the metric without serving the goal
- Nobody able to articulate what outcome the metric was originally a proxy for

## Countermeasures

- Pair every proxy metric with the downstream outcome it serves, reviewed together
- Audit metrics annually: is this still measuring what we care about?
- Tie incentives to outcome metrics, or to balanced sets that resist gaming

## Marketing example

The team is bonused on MQL volume. Forms get shortened, scoring thresholds loosened, gated content multiplies. MQLs triple. Sales accepts 10% of them, pipeline stays flat, and the sales-marketing relationship deteriorates into a dispute about lead definitions. The metric won; the goal lost.
