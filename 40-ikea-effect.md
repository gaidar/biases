# 40 – IKEA effect

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

Overvaluing things because you built them yourself. Effort invested inflates perceived quality.

## Impact on decisions

Creators defend their work against evidence. Homegrown campaigns, decks, and assets get judged by the labor behind them rather than the results in front of them. Testing that threatens self-built work gets discounted or skipped.

## Detection signals

- The builder is also the evaluator
- Effort and craft cited in defense of underperforming work
- Test results against self-built work challenged on methodology, favorable results accepted at face value

## Countermeasures

- Separate creation from evaluation – different people, ideally different teams
- A/B test self-built work against alternatives as standard practice
- Judge assets on performance metrics agreed before the work started

## Marketing example

The team spends five months developing a new tagline. Message testing shows the old tagline outperforms it on recall and preference. The team challenges the test methodology, reruns it with friendlier framing, and ships the new tagline anyway. The five months of effort, not the evidence, made the decision.
