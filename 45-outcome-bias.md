# 45 – Outcome bias

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

Judging the quality of a decision by its result rather than by the quality of the process and the information available at the time.

## Impact on decisions

Lucky gambles get rewarded and copied; sound bets that failed get punished. Over time, the organization selects for risk-blind behavior that has not yet blown up, and drives out disciplined decision-making.

## Detection signals

- "It worked, didn't it?" ending process discussions
- Promotions and budget following results with no examination of method
- Well-reasoned decisions punished after unlucky outcomes

## Countermeasures

- Review decision process explicitly: what was known, what was considered, how was risk sized?
- Reward good process publicly, including when the outcome disappointed
- Separate outcome reviews from decision-quality reviews in postmortems

## Marketing example

A manager skips testing and bets the quarter's budget on a single campaign. It happens to land, she is promoted, and the no-testing approach spreads as "decisiveness." The following year, two teams applying the same approach lose their quarters. The original decision was bad when it was made; the outcome hid it.
