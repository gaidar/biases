# 32 – Bandwagon effect

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

Adopting beliefs, tools, or tactics because others are adopting them, with adoption itself treated as evidence.

## Impact on decisions

Trend-chasing consumes budget and attention. Tactics get copied without checking fit for the specific audience, product, or economics. The question "does everyone do this?" replaces "does this work for us?"

## Detection signals

- "Everyone in the industry is doing X" as the primary justification
- No first-principles case for fit with your buyers
- Adoption decisions timed to conference seasons

## Countermeasures

- Require a fit check: which of our buyers, at what stage, does this reach?
- Pilot small with defined success criteria before scaling
- Remember the survivor problem: you see the adopters, not the abandoners

## Marketing example

Every competitor launches a podcast, so the team does too. Fourteen episodes average 90 listens each; none produce measurable pipeline. The podcast consumed a content manager's full quarter and dies quietly. The buyers, it turns out, read analyst reports and peer reviews – channels that got no incremental investment that quarter.
