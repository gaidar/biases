# 27 – Zero-risk bias

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Paying disproportionately to eliminate a small residual risk entirely, while larger risks remain partially or wholly unmanaged.

## Impact on decisions

Review processes, insurance, and controls pile onto trivial risks because "zero" feels categorically better than "low." The big, uncomfortable risks – which cannot be zeroed – get less attention than they deserve.

## Detection signals

- Heavy process around minor risks, informal handling of major ones
- Risk discussions about elimination rather than expected impact
- Cost of a control exceeding the expected loss it prevents

## Countermeasures

- Rank risks by probability times impact; allocate control effort accordingly
- Price every control against the expected loss it prevents
- Accept explicitly that some small risks are cheaper to carry than to remove

## Marketing example

Legal reviews every social post, adding two weeks of delay to eliminate a minor compliance risk that has never materialized. Meanwhile, partner co-branding – where partners use the logo and make product claims with no review at all – carries the real regulatory exposure and remains unmanaged.
