# 11 – Framing effect

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

The same facts produce different decisions depending on how they are worded – as gains or losses, percentages or absolutes, success or failure.

## Impact on decisions

Program reviews, budget requests, and vendor pitches get approved or rejected based on presentation, not substance. Whoever writes the slide controls the outcome more than the data does.

## Detection signals

- The decision flips when the metric is restated (90% retention vs 10% churn)
- Percentages presented without absolute numbers, or vice versa, selectively
- Proposals framed only as gains, risks framed only as losses

## Countermeasures

- Restate every key figure in both frames before deciding
- Standardize dashboard definitions so framing cannot be chosen per meeting
- Require absolute numbers next to every percentage

## Marketing example

A campaign review states "we reached 95% of target CPL" and the program gets renewed. Reframed as "CPL ran 5% over budget while lead-to-opportunity conversion fell 30%," the same program would have been cut. The frame, not the performance, made the decision.
