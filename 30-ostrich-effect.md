# 30 – Ostrich effect

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Avoiding information that is likely to be negative – not looking at the dashboard after the bad launch.

## Impact on decisions

Failures go unexamined, so their causes repeat. Postmortems get delayed until memories fade and stakes cool. Metrics get reviewed enthusiastically when good and skipped when bad, producing a distorted organizational memory.

## Detection signals

- Readouts postponed after poor results
- Dashboards checked daily during good streaks, weekly during bad ones
- Postmortems that finally happen are softened or reframed

## Countermeasures

- Fix review schedules that run regardless of results
- Make postmortems blameless so bad news carries no personal price
- Track review completion as a process metric

## Marketing example

A $200K campaign flops. The readout gets postponed twice, then presented with attribution windows adjusted to soften the miss. No root cause is established. Next quarter the same channel and format get funded again and produce the same result.
