# 55 – Scarcity bias

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

Things framed as limited – in quantity or time – get valued more highly and decided on faster, regardless of underlying worth.

## Impact on decisions

Deadline pressure replaces evaluation. Vendors manufacture urgency ("last slot," "expires Friday") and buyers compress diligence to meet artificial clocks. Rushed commitments at full price.

## Detection signals

- The vendor's deadline, not your process, sets the decision timeline
- Scarcity claims accepted without verification
- Discomfort at the idea of simply letting the "opportunity" pass

## Countermeasures

- Decide on pre-set criteria; if the deadline prevents proper evaluation, the answer is no
- Verify scarcity claims – ask directly, check with peers, wait and watch
- Maintain walk-away discipline: real opportunities usually survive a week of diligence

## Marketing example

A media vendor claims the last premium sponsorship slot expires in 48 hours. The team signs at $120K without benchmarking comparable placements. A month later, a peer company receives the same "last slot" offer. The scarcity was a sales tactic; the 48-hour clock cost the team roughly $30K in unnegotiated premium.
