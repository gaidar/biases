# 29 – Mental accounting

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Treating identical money differently depending on which budget label it carries.

## Impact on decisions

High-return opportunities go unfunded because "that line is spent" while low-return spend proceeds because "that line has room." Use-it-or-lose-it dynamics produce December waste. A dollar is a dollar; the labels say otherwise.

## Detection signals

- "That's a different budget" blocking an obviously better use of money
- Year-end spending sprees to protect next year's allocation
- ROI never compared across budget lines, only within them

## Countermeasures

- Run periodic fungibility reviews: rank all spend by return regardless of line
- Allow mid-year reallocation with a lightweight approval path
- Kill use-it-or-lose-it by basing next year's budget on plans, not on prior consumption

## Marketing example

In December, the events line has $90K left and digital is exhausted. The team books a low-value sponsorship to consume the events line while a digital opportunity with double the expected pipeline goes unfunded. Both decisions optimize budget labels; neither optimizes the business.
