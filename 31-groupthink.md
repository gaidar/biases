# 31 – Groupthink

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

The drive for group consensus suppresses dissent, doubt, and alternatives. Cohesion becomes more important than being right.

## Impact on decisions

Plans sail through review unchallenged. Known risks stay unspoken because raising them reads as negativity. Everyone privately doubts; nobody speaks; the group is confident.

## Detection signals

- Fast, unanimous agreement on complex decisions
- Dissent labeled as "not being a team player"
- After failures, multiple people admit they had concerns

## Countermeasures

- Collect written, anonymous input before group discussion
- Assign a rotating devil's advocate with real preparation time
- Leader states their view last, not first
- Ask directly: "What is the strongest argument against this plan?"

## Marketing example

At the campaign kickoff, the CMO opens by presenting a favored concept. Three people privately doubt the audience fit; none speak. The meeting ends in enthusiastic consensus. The campaign underperforms, and in the postmortem all three say they "had a feeling" – information that was free at kickoff and expensive now.
