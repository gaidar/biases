# 06 – Overconfidence

**Category:** A – Estimation and forecasting

## Definition

Overestimating the accuracy of your own knowledge and forecasts. Includes overprecision (ranges too narrow) and overplacement (believing you are better than peers).

## Impact on decisions

Point forecasts replace ranges, tests get skipped because "we know this will work," and contingency plans never get built. Real spend gets committed against numbers that carry hidden variance.

## Detection signals

- Forecasts stated as single numbers with no range or confidence interval
- "I'm sure" or "definitely" attached to inherently uncertain outcomes
- No track record kept of past forecast accuracy

## Countermeasures

- Force ranges: best case, expected, worst case, with rough probabilities
- Track forecast vs actual over time and review calibration quarterly
- Run premortems before committing spend
- Size bets so a wrong forecast does not sink the quarter

## Marketing example

The team forecasts 1,200 registrations for a flagship event, stated as a single number. Venue, catering, and staffing – $180K – get committed against it. Actual registrations: 640. A range forecast (500-1,100) would have driven a phased commitment and saved roughly $60K.
