# 28 – Present bias

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Overweighting near-term payoffs relative to larger later ones – hyperbolic discounting applied to business choices.

## Impact on decisions

Short-payback activation consistently beats long-payback brand and infrastructure work in budget fights. Quarter-end discounting trades margin for timing. Long-term projects get perpetually deferred one more quarter.

## Detection signals

- Long-term investments lose every head-to-head budget comparison
- "Next quarter" repeated for the same project across multiple quarters
- Rising acquisition costs attributed to competition rather than to years of underinvestment upstream

## Countermeasures

- Protect long-term budget lines structurally; do not make them compete with in-quarter lead-gen
- Use commitment devices: multi-quarter approvals that require active cancellation
- Track leading indicators (awareness, share of search) so long-term work has visible progress

## Marketing example

Brand-building loses the budget fight to lead-gen every quarter for three years because lead-gen pays back in ninety days. Over those three years CPCs double: the company bids cold against competitors whose unaided awareness grew while its own stayed flat. The "expensive" brand investment would have been the cheap option.
