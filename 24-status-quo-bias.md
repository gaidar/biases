# 24 – Status quo bias

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Preferring the current state of affairs regardless of merit. Includes the default effect: whatever requires no decision keeps happening.

## Impact on decisions

Legacy programs auto-renew. Vendor contracts roll over unexamined. Annual plans copy last year with cosmetic adjustments. Change carries a burden of proof that continuity never faces.

## Detection signals

- "We've always done it this way" as a closing argument
- Renewals processed without a performance review
- The annual plan is last year's plan plus 5%

## Countermeasures

- Zero-based budgeting: every program justifies itself from zero, periodically
- Default to sunset: programs expire unless actively renewed with evidence
- Apply the same burden of proof to continuing as to changing

## Marketing example

The annual plan copies last year's channel mix with 5% adjustments. Three of eight channels have produced no attributable pipeline in two years but survive as incumbents. Meanwhile a proposal for one new channel gets grilled for an hour on projected ROI – a standard the incumbents were never held to.
