# 13 – Salience bias

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Vivid, dramatic, or emotionally striking information gets more weight than dull but important data.

## Impact on decisions

A single memorable customer complaint reshapes the roadmap. An executive's personal observation outweighs a quarter of attribution data. Boring high-performers lose budget to exciting low-performers.

## Detection signals

- One anecdote cited more often than any dataset in decision meetings
- "The CEO saw..." as the origin of a budget line
- Unglamorous channels underfunded despite leading measured performance

## Countermeasures

- Weight inputs by frequency and revenue impact, not memorability
- Convert anecdotes into hypotheses and test them against data
- Review the full performance table before discussing any single story

## Marketing example

The CEO sees a competitor billboard at an airport and asks why the company has no out-of-home presence. A $250K airport campaign gets funded. Attribution data shows 70% of pipeline comes from SEO and lifecycle email – both flat-funded that year because nobody tells stories about them.
