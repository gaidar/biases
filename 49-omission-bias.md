# 49 – Omission bias

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

Harm caused by inaction gets judged more leniently than equal harm caused by action. Not deciding feels safe because it leaves no fingerprints.

## Impact on decisions

Missed opportunities escape review entirely – there is no failed project to postmortem. Risky non-decisions accumulate silently while every action faces scrutiny. The organization systematically underinvests in anything requiring a positive decision.

## Detection signals

- No process reviews missed opportunities, only failed actions
- "We didn't do anything wrong" after a competitor takes ground unopposed
- Repeated deferral treated as neutral rather than as a decision

## Countermeasures

- Log explicit decisions not to act, with reasoning and an owner
- Review missed opportunities annually with the same rigor as failed projects
- Treat "defer" as a decision with a cost, not as the absence of one

## Marketing example

For two years, nobody decides to enter an emerging channel – it just never gets prioritized. A competitor builds a dominant position there and sources a third of its new pipeline from it. No postmortem happens, because no one made a mistake anyone can point to. The inaction cost more than any failed campaign that year, and nobody owns it.
