# 41 – Fundamental attribution error

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

Explaining other people's outcomes by their character and ability while underweighting their circumstances, constraints, and systems.

## Impact on decisions

Individuals get blamed for system failures. People get fired or sidelined when the process, resourcing, or territory was the problem – and the problem persists under their replacement.

## Detection signals

- Performance problems explained by traits ("not driven," "not strategic")
- No analysis of resources, territory, or process behind a miss
- The same role underperforming across multiple different people

## Countermeasures

- Examine the system first: what would a strong performer achieve under these exact constraints?
- Compare performance across contexts before attributing to the person
- In reviews, require a circumstances section alongside the individual assessment

## Marketing example

A field marketer misses pipeline targets and goes on a performance plan. Her region lost its SDR support mid-quarter and its event budget was cut 40% – neither appears in the review. Her successor, with the same constraints, misses by more. Two quarters and one good employee were spent not fixing the actual problem.
