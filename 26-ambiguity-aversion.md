# 26 – Ambiguity aversion

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Preferring known risks over unknown ones, even when the unknown option has better expected value.

## Impact on decisions

Measured, declining channels keep their budgets because their poor returns are at least predictable. Unmeasured but promising options never get tested because "we have no data" – which is exactly why a test would be valuable.

## Detection signals

- "We don't have data on that" ends the discussion instead of starting a test
- Budget concentrated in channels with declining but well-documented returns
- Information-buying experiments never proposed

## Countermeasures

- Treat small experiments as purchases of information, with a price and expected value
- Compare options on expected value ranges, not on data availability
- Reserve a fixed percentage of budget for testing unknowns

## Marketing example

Paid search ROI has declined 20% year over year, but it is measurable, so it keeps its $600K budget. A $10K test in an emerging channel gets rejected twice for lack of data. A competitor runs the test, finds CPLs at a third of search, and builds a two-year head start.
