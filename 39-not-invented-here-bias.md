# 39 – Not-invented-here bias

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

Rejecting external ideas, tools, and solutions because they came from outside – dressed up as "our situation is unique."

## Impact on decisions

Teams rebuild what could be bought, at multiples of the cost. External best practice gets dismissed without evaluation. Build-vs-buy analysis either does not happen or gets rigged toward build.

## Detection signals

- "Our situation is unique" without specifics on what is actually unique
- Build decisions made without a costed buy alternative
- External tools evaluated on their gaps, internal builds on their promises

## Countermeasures

- Require a build-vs-buy analysis with total cost of ownership, including maintenance headcount
- Pilot the external option first; build only for the gap that remains
- Apply symmetric scrutiny: list the internal build's gaps as rigorously as the vendor's

## Marketing example

The ops team spends six months building a custom attribution model. An off-the-shelf tool at $30K per year covers 90% of the need. The custom model works, and permanently consumes one full-time analyst – roughly $120K per year – to maintain the remaining 10% of differentiation nobody uses.
