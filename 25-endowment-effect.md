# 25 – Endowment effect

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Overvaluing things simply because you own or built them, relative to what you would pay to acquire them fresh.

## Impact on decisions

Owned assets – tools, properties, content libraries, brands – get maintained past their economic life. Internal valuations run above what any outside buyer or user would pay.

## Detection signals

- Maintenance budgets for assets nobody would build today
- Resistance to sunsetting framed as protecting "what we've built"
- Internal valuation never tested against market alternatives

## Countermeasures

- Apply the ownership test: "Would we buy or build this today at this cost?"
- Get outside valuations for significant owned assets
- Review the full asset inventory annually with sunset as the default for underperformers

## Marketing example

The team maintains a campaign microsite at $80K per year in hosting, updates, and agency retainer. Asked directly whether they would build it today for $80K, everyone says no. It survives four more budget cycles because nobody proposes killing something "we invested so much in."
