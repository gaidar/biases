# 15 – Narrative fallacy

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Forcing coherent cause-and-effect stories onto noisy or coincidental data because stories are easier to remember and believe than statistics.

## Impact on decisions

Quarterly results get wrapped in tidy explanations that drive next quarter's plan, even when the real drivers were external, random, or unknown. Strategy gets built on the story, not the data.

## Detection signals

- Every fluctuation has a confident causal explanation
- The explanation was produced after the result and never tested
- Alternative explanations are absent from the readout

## Countermeasures

- For every causal claim, demand the data and the mechanism
- List at least two alternative explanations, including "chance"
- Test the story's prediction on the next period before funding it

## Marketing example

Q3 pipeline grows 30% and the readout credits "our new messaging resonating with the market." The same quarter, a major competitor exited the region and prices dropped 15%. The messaging story wins, the team doubles content budget, and Q4 – with the competitor effect absorbed – comes in flat.
