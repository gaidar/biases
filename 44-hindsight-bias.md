# 44 – Hindsight bias

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

After an outcome is known, it feels like it was predictable all along – "we knew it." Memory of prior uncertainty gets rewritten.

## Impact on decisions

Decision-makers get judged unfairly against knowledge that did not exist at decision time. Wrong lessons get institutionalized because the clean retrospective story replaces the messy real one. Risk-taking gets punished retroactively.

## Detection signals

- "It was obvious" said about outcomes nobody predicted in writing
- Retrospectives with suspiciously clean causal narratives
- No documentation of what was actually known and forecast at decision time

## Countermeasures

- Keep decision journals: record the forecast, reasoning, and known unknowns before outcomes
- Judge decisions against the information available at the time, on the record
- In postmortems, start from the written pre-decision documents, not from memory

## Marketing example

After a failed product launch, leadership declares the timing problems "obvious from the start." Someone retrieves the pre-launch risk review: timing was not flagged by anyone, including the current critics. Without that document, the company would have institutionalized a lesson about a risk nobody actually foresaw – and missed the real cause.
