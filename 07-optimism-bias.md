# 07 – Optimism bias

**Category:** A – Estimation and forecasting

## Definition

Systematically expecting outcomes to be better than the evidence supports – for yourself and your projects specifically.

## Impact on decisions

Plans contain only favorable scenarios. Revenue projections use above-historical conversion rates. Downside cases exist as a slide, never as a funded contingency. Commitments cascade from numbers nobody stress-tested.

## Detection signals

- Every assumption in the model sits at or above historical averages
- The "conservative" scenario still beats last year's actuals
- Risk sections in plans are generic, short, or missing

## Countermeasures

- Anchor every assumption to your own historical actuals, then justify deviations
- Build and cost a genuine downside scenario, not a cosmetic one
- Have someone outside the project review the assumptions
- Run a premortem before sign-off

## Marketing example

A launch plan assumes 5% landing-page conversion. The team's historical average across 14 campaigns is 1.8%. Revenue commitments to the board get built on the 5% figure. The launch converts at 2.1% – a good result by any historical measure – and is declared a failure.
