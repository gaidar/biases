# 20 – Curse of knowledge

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Once you know something, you cannot accurately imagine not knowing it. Experts systematically overestimate what their audience understands.

## Impact on decisions

Customer-facing content gets written in insider language. Briefs assume context the agency does not have. Onboarding, sales decks, and websites make sense only to the people who built them.

## Detection signals

- Internal acronyms and product jargon in external content
- "Everyone knows what this product does" from people who built it
- New employees or outsiders consistently confused by materials insiders consider clear

## Countermeasures

- Test all external content on people with zero product exposure
- Have someone outside the team read every brief before it goes out
- Rewrite until a smart person from a different industry gets it in one pass

## Marketing example

Product marketing writes homepage copy dense with internal acronyms and category terms the company invented. New-visitor bounce rate sits at 78%. An outside writer with no product background rewrites it in plain language; bounce drops to 55% and demo requests rise 20%.
