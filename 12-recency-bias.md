# 12 – Recency bias

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Overweighting the most recent data point or event relative to the longer trend.

## Impact on decisions

Strategy whiplash: last week's numbers dominate quarterly reviews, one bad result kills programs with solid long-term records, one good week scales unproven tactics.

## Detection signals

- Strategy discussions anchored on the latest week or month
- Program decisions reversed shortly after a single unusual result
- Trend lines absent from review decks; only latest-period snapshots shown

## Countermeasures

- Review rolling averages (13-week, 12-month) next to latest figures
- Set minimum evaluation windows before program decisions
- Fix a decision cadence and refuse mid-cycle reversals without new structural information

## Marketing example

One webinar draws 40% below average attendance. In the next pipeline meeting the series gets cancelled – despite being the second-best pipeline source over the trailing year. The weak session coincided with a public holiday in two major markets, visible in one glance at the trend line nobody pulled up.
