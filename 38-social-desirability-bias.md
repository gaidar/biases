# 38 – Social desirability bias

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

People report what sounds good – to the interviewer, the group, or their self-image – rather than what is true. It distorts surveys, focus groups, and internal feedback alike.

## Impact on decisions

Stated intentions get treated as demand forecasts. Customers claim they will pay, employees claim they are aligned, prospects claim price is not the issue. Plans built on stated preferences collide with revealed behavior.

## Detection signals

- Survey results notably rosier than behavioral data
- Focus groups praising concepts that later fail in market
- Willingness-to-pay questions asked directly

## Countermeasures

- Weight behavioral data (purchases, usage, clicks) over stated data
- Use indirect questioning and trade-off methods (e.g., forced choices) instead of direct claims
- Pilot with real transactions before scaling on survey evidence

## Marketing example

A survey finds 70% of customers would pay for a premium tier. Roadmap and revenue plan get built on it. At launch, 4% convert. Saying yes in a survey cost respondents nothing; the plan treated free words as committed dollars.
