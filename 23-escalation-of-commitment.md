# 23 – Escalation of commitment

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Increasing investment in a failing course of action to justify prior decisions – the behavioral pattern that sunk cost thinking produces, amplified by ego and public commitment.

## Impact on decisions

Bets grow after misses instead of shrinking. The person who made the original call keeps doubling to avoid admitting error. Success criteria quietly get redefined downward to keep the project alive.

## Detection signals

- Budget increases follow missed targets
- Original decision-maker controls the continue/kill call
- Goalposts have moved since the project started

## Countermeasures

- Set kill criteria in writing before launch
- Move continue/kill decisions to someone without authorship of the original bet
- Use stage gates with fresh approval required at each stage
- Reward well-run kills publicly

## Marketing example

A paid channel misses CPL targets three months straight. The manager who championed it raises budget each month – $40K, $60K, $85K – arguing it "needs scale to perform." Kill criteria were never written. Total loss by quarter end: $185K, plus three months of a channel that was never going to work.
