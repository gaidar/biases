# 48 – Action bias

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

Doing something feels better than doing nothing, even when inaction has higher expected value. Visible activity substitutes for judgment.

## Impact on decisions

Reactions get launched before analysis. "We must respond" replaces "should we respond?" Costs pile up from moves whose only function was to demonstrate motion.

## Detection signals

- Responses launched within days of a competitor move, analysis to follow
- "Do nothing" never appears as an evaluated option
- Activity metrics healthy, outcome metrics flat

## Countermeasures

- Put "do nothing" on every option list with its own expected value
- Impose a short analysis gate before reactive spend above a threshold
- Reward outcomes, not visible busyness

## Marketing example

A competitor cuts prices. Within a week the team ships a matching discount campaign. Analysis completed afterward shows the competitor's cut targeted a segment with almost no customer overlap. The discount cost $200K in margin and provided zero defensive value – but everyone agreed the team "moved fast."
