# 19 – Focusing illusion

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Whatever you are currently paying attention to seems more important than it is. Nothing matters as much as it does while you are thinking about it.

## Impact on decisions

Single metrics or issues absorb entire quarters while the rest of the system degrades unnoticed. The current obsession crowds out portfolio thinking.

## Detection signals

- One number dominates every meeting for weeks
- Improvements in the focus metric coincide with silent declines elsewhere
- Nobody can name what the team stopped watching

## Countermeasures

- Review a balanced set of metrics on a fixed cadence, even when one is on fire
- Explicitly list what is being deprioritized and accept it consciously
- Rotate the spotlight: schedule reviews of neglected areas

## Marketing example

Leadership fixates on MQL volume for a quarter. Volume doubles through looser scoring and more gated content. MQL-to-opportunity conversion halves, sales acceptance drops, net pipeline stays flat, and spend rises 40%. Every meeting tracked the one number that was improving.
