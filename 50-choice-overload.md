# 50 – Choice overload

**Category:** F – Action and decision structure

## Definition

Too many options degrade decision quality or delay the decision indefinitely – analysis paralysis in practice.

## Impact on decisions

Long-lists never become shortlists. Evaluations run so long that requirements change and the process restarts. The cost of not deciding exceeds the difference between the top options.

## Detection signals

- Vendor or option lists above five that are not shrinking
- Decisions postponed to gather still more options
- Evaluation timelines measured in quarters for choices of moderate stakes

## Countermeasures

- Shortlist to three fast, using coarse criteria; spend real effort only on finalists
- Satisfice deliberately: define "good enough" criteria and take the first option that clears them
- Set decision deadlines with a named owner empowered to choose

## Marketing example

A martech selection starts with fourteen vendors and runs nine months of comparative scoring. By decision time, requirements have shifted and two shortlisted vendors changed pricing, so the evaluation restarts. The team runs another year on spreadsheets. The difference between the top three tools was marginal; the delay was not.
