# 14 – Survivorship bias

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Drawing lessons only from visible successes because failures disappeared from view.

## Impact on decisions

Strategies get copied from famous winners without knowing how many companies tried the same and failed. Best-practice content, conference talks, and case studies are a survivor-only sample by construction.

## Detection signals

- "Look what worked for [famous company]" as the core argument
- No attempt to find companies that tried the tactic and failed
- Internal case studies exist only for wins

## Countermeasures

- Ask: "How many tried this? What happened to the rest?"
- Study your own failed campaigns with the same rigor as wins
- Discount any evidence set that could not, by construction, contain failures

## Marketing example

The team copies a famous consumer brand's provocative campaign style. Thousands of companies attempted similar tones and failed invisibly; only the survivor is in the conference keynote. The tone lands wrong for risk-averse B2B buyers and burns two quarters of brand budget.
