# 01 – Anchoring

**Category:** A – Estimation and forecasting

## Definition

The first number encountered pulls all subsequent estimates toward it, even when that number is arbitrary or irrelevant.

## Impact on decisions

Budgets, prices, and forecasts get negotiated relative to an initial figure instead of built from actual value or cost. Whoever states the first number controls the range of the discussion. Last year's budget becomes the anchor for this year's, regardless of whether the underlying work changed.

## Detection signals

- Negotiations move in small steps away from the first proposal
- "Last year we spent X" opens every budget conversation
- Nobody produced an independent estimate before seeing someone else's number

## Countermeasures

- Prepare your own estimate before seeing any proposal or prior-year figure
- Use zero-based budgeting for programs that renew automatically
- Collect several independent estimates and compare, rather than debating one
- In negotiations, state your number first when you have solid grounds for it

## Marketing example

An agency proposes $400K for a campaign. The team negotiates hard and lands at $340K, feeling like they saved $60K. Comparable scope from two other agencies costs $220K. The $400K anchor defined the entire negotiation; nobody priced the work bottom-up.
