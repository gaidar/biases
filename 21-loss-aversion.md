# 21 – Loss aversion

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Losses hurt roughly twice as much as equivalent gains feel good, so decisions skew toward avoiding losses over pursuing gains.

## Impact on decisions

Failing programs survive because killing them registers as a loss. Defensive moves get funded over growth bets with better expected value. Positions get protected long after they stopped earning their cost.

## Detection signals

- "We can't give up our position in X" without a return calculation
- Reallocation proposals framed and felt as losses, not swaps
- Defensive spend growing while growth experiments starve

## Countermeasures

- Reframe every hold decision as a purchase: "Would we buy this position today at this price?"
- Compare expected value across options, not comfort
- Manage programs as a portfolio where reallocations are routine, not defeats

## Marketing example

The team keeps a $150K legacy trade show sponsorship because dropping it "loses our presence." Measured pipeline from the show has declined three years running. The same budget in a partner-marketing program returns three times more on measured pipeline, but the swap feels like a loss and never happens.
