# 43 – Dunning-Kruger effect

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

Low competence in a domain pairs with high confidence, because the skills needed to perform well are the same skills needed to recognize poor performance.

## Impact on decisions

Confident novices make consequential calls in domains they entered last month. Their certainty reads as competence to non-experts, so the calls go unchallenged until the damage surfaces.

## Detection signals

- Sweeping confidence in a domain the person recently entered
- No error bars, caveats, or awareness of known failure modes
- Actual experts in the room notably more hesitant than the novice

## Countermeasures

- Weight confidence by demonstrated track record in that specific domain
- Require second opinions from practitioners for consequential technical decisions
- Treat "this is simple" about a mature discipline as a warning sign

## Marketing example

A demand-gen manager, confident after a weekend SEO course, rewrites the site's technical SEO strategy solo. Misconfigured redirects and noindex tags deindex key pages. Organic traffic drops 40% and takes six months to recover. An hour of review from an experienced SEO would have caught every mistake.
