# 16 – Illusory correlation

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Perceiving a relationship between two variables that does not exist, or mistaking correlation for causation when it does.

## Impact on decisions

False drivers get funded. Activities that co-occur with success get credited for causing it, and budget follows the coincidence.

## Detection signals

- Correlation presented as causation with no controls
- No mechanism offered for why X would cause Y
- The claimed relationship reverses plausibly (Y could cause X)

## Countermeasures

- Ask which direction the causality runs and what the mechanism is
- Use holdout groups or natural experiments before scaling
- Check whether a third factor drives both variables

## Marketing example

Analysis shows deals with whitepaper downloads close twice as often. The team triples whitepaper production and promotion. Closing rates do not move: late-stage buyers download whitepapers as part of final validation. The downloads were a symptom of buying intent, not a cause of it.
