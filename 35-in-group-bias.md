# 35 – In-group bias

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

Favoring your own team, function, network, or established vendor relationships over outsiders, independent of merit.

## Impact on decisions

Incumbent vendors win without competition. Ideas from inside the circle get a friendly hearing; identical ideas from outside get dissected. Personal networks quietly become procurement policy.

## Detection signals

- Significant contracts renewed without competitive bids
- External proposals held to a visibly higher standard than internal ones
- Vendor relationships that trace to personal history rather than selection

## Countermeasures

- Run structured RFPs for significant spend on a fixed cycle
- Evaluate ideas blind where feasible
- Benchmark incumbent vendor rates against market every renewal

## Marketing example

Creative work always goes to the agency founded by the CMO's former colleagues. No competitive review in four years. A benchmarking exercise finally shows rates 30% above market and performance in the middle of the pack. The relationship was comfortable; the premium was real.
