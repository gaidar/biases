# 46 – Illusion of control

**Category:** E – Self-assessment and attribution

## Definition

Overestimating how much your actions influence outcomes that are substantially driven by external factors or chance.

## Impact on decisions

Teams take credit for results the market delivered and scale activities that were along for the ride. Incrementality – what would have happened anyway – never gets measured, so budgets flow to activities with zero real effect.

## Detection signals

- Results attributed entirely to own actions, external drivers unmentioned
- No holdout or incrementality testing anywhere in the measurement stack
- Confidence that "we can hit the number" independent of market conditions

## Countermeasures

- Run holdout tests on major always-on programs to measure true incrementality
- Include market factors (demand trends, competitor moves) in every performance narrative
- Distinguish influenced metrics from controlled metrics in target-setting

## Marketing example

The team credits its always-on paid program for steady lead flow and doubles the budget. A holdout test in two regions shows 80% of those leads arrive anyway through direct and organic demand – the ads were harvesting existing intent, not creating it. The doubled budget buys almost nothing incremental.
