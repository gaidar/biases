# 22 – Sunk cost fallacy

**Category:** C – Risk, value, and money

## Definition

Letting money, time, or effort already spent – and unrecoverable – influence decisions about future spend.

## Impact on decisions

Failing projects get continued "so the investment isn't wasted." The only rational inputs to a go-forward decision are incremental cost and incremental benefit; sunk costs poison both.

## Detection signals

- "We've already invested X" cited as a reason to continue
- Project reviews that tally past spend instead of future return
- Nobody modeling the alternative use of the remaining budget

## Countermeasures

- Evaluate only: what does it cost from here, and what do we get from here?
- Bring in a reviewer with no history on the project
- Ask: "If we were starting today with this budget, would we choose this?"

## Marketing example

$300K into a marketing automation migration, the platform clearly does not fit the use case. The team approves another $200K to "not waste" the first $300K. A fit-for-purpose alternative would cost $180K total from this point. The sunk $300K is gone either way; the decision spends $20K extra to protect a feeling.
