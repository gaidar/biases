# 18 – Information bias

**Category:** B – Information processing

## Definition

Gathering more data that cannot change the decision – research as a substitute for deciding.

## Impact on decisions

Decisions stall behind "one more study." Money and time go into analysis whose results have no path to altering the plan. Windows close while the team collects.

## Detection signals

- Nobody can state what result would change the decision
- Research commissioned after the decision is effectively made
- Repeated requests for more data with no decision criteria attached

## Countermeasures

- Before any study, write down: "If the result is X we do A; if Y we do B." If all paths lead to A, skip the study
- Set decision deadlines that expire regardless of pending analysis
- Price the delay: what does each month of waiting cost?

## Marketing example

The CEO has approved a positioning change. The team commissions a $60K brand tracker "to validate direction" before executing. No possible result would reverse an approved decision. The study delays launch by a quarter and its findings are never referenced again.
