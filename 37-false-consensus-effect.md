# 37 – False consensus effect

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

Assuming other people share your preferences, habits, and reactions more than they actually do.

## Impact on decisions

The team's own tastes get projected onto customers. Content formats, channels, and messages get chosen by what the marketers themselves would respond to, not what the buyers respond to.

## Detection signals

- "Nobody reads long emails" and similar universal claims with no data
- Creative decisions justified by the team's personal reactions
- Audience demographics sharply different from team demographics, unacknowledged

## Countermeasures

- Test with actual customers before generalizing from the room
- When someone says "customers obviously want X," ask which customers said so
- Review audience research quarterly to keep the real buyer in view

## Marketing example

A team of marketers in their late twenties kills long-form email because "no one reads long emails." Their audience – IT directors in their fifties – engages more with detailed technical content, visible in historical click data nobody consulted. After the switch to short punchy emails, meetings booked from the nurture program drop by a third.
