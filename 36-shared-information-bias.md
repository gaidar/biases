# 36 – Shared information bias

**Category:** D – Social and group dynamics

## Definition

Groups spend their time discussing what everyone already knows and neglect information held by only one member – which is usually the information that could change the decision.

## Impact on decisions

Meetings rehash common knowledge and feel productive. Unique field observations, customer signals, and specialist knowledge never enter the discussion. Decisions get made on the shared subset, which is the least valuable subset.

## Detection signals

- Meetings dominated by facts already in everyone's dashboard
- Quiet specialists rarely asked for what only they know
- Post-decision discoveries of "someone actually knew that"

## Countermeasures

- Collect written input from each participant before the meeting
- Explicitly ask each person: "What do you know that others here might not?"
- Structure agendas around open questions, not around restating known data

## Marketing example

The QBR spends forty minutes on the CPL trend everyone has watched all quarter. The field marketer's unique observation – a competitor winning deals on onboarding speed, heard from six prospects – never surfaces. Two quarters later it shows up in win-loss analysis, after the competitor took eight deals.
