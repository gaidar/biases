# 03 – Representativeness heuristic

**Category:** A – Estimation and forecasting

## Definition

Judging probability by how much something resembles a stereotype or prototype, instead of by data about the actual population.

## Impact on decisions

Personas, hires, and target accounts get selected because they "look like" the expected profile. Real customer composition gets ignored. Money flows to segments that match the mental image, not the revenue.

## Detection signals

- "Our typical customer is..." stated without data behind it
- Campaigns designed for a persona nobody has validated recently
- Candidates or vendors chosen because they resemble past successes

## Countermeasures

- Rebuild personas from CRM and win data annually, not from memory
- Check the actual distribution before targeting the stereotype
- When someone pattern-matches, ask for the base rate

## Marketing example

The team designs a campaign for the stereotypical "young startup CTO" – memes, dev-culture references, product-led tone. Win data shows 60% of actual buyers are operations managers at 200-person companies who care about compliance and support SLAs. The campaign gets engagement from the wrong audience and conversion drops.
